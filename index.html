About

Research at the intersection of
economics, policy, and health systems

I am a biostatistician and health economist at the Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital of Chicago, Department of Pediatrics, where I work across two research programs. In collaborative clinical research, I focus on pediatric cardiology and congenital heart disease outcomes, using linked administrative data and competing risks models to examine disparities in diagnosis, intervention timing, and perinatal regionalization.

My independent research agenda spans two fields: the industrial organization of pharmaceutical markets — with a focus on patent strategy, regulatory design, and generic entry under the Hatch-Waxman Act — and maternal health access, where I examine the structural determinants of obstetric service availability, maternity care deserts, and geographic barriers to prenatal care.

My methodological toolkit includes structural equation modeling, generalized structural equation models, panel econometrics, Fine-Gray competing risks regression, and geospatial analysis. I hold a Ph.D. in Economics from the University of Illinois at Chicago, where my dissertation examined pharmaceutical firm behavior under the Hatch-Waxman regulatory framework.

Research Programs

Four research programs united by a focus on
how institutions and systems shape health

Pharmaceutical Industrial Organization

I study how regulatory architecture shapes strategic behavior in pharmaceutical markets. My work examines patent portfolio design, Orange Book listing strategy, and the timing of generic entry under the Hatch-Waxman Act, connecting regulatory design to market outcomes for generic competition.

  • Hatch-Waxman Act
  • Generic Entry Timing
  • Patent Strategy
  • Regulatory Design
  • Orange Book
  • Protection Regimes

Maternal Health Access & Obstetric Systems

I examine the structural determinants of obstetric service availability across U.S. counties, developing frameworks that distinguish feasibility from vulnerability in hospital-based obstetric care. Related work addresses maternity care deserts, prenatal care timing, and geographic referral burden.

  • Maternity Care Deserts
  • Obstetric Service Regimes
  • Prenatal Care Access
  • Geographic Barriers
  • Rural Health
  • Perinatal Regionalization

Congenital Heart Disease Outcomes

In collaborative work with the Lurie Heart Center, I study disparities in the care of children with congenital heart defects — with a focus on prenatal diagnosis, insurance-based differences in intervention timing, length of stay, and perinatal regionalization for critical CHD.

  • Prenatal Diagnosis
  • CHD Outcomes
  • Insurance Disparities
  • Competing Risks
  • Perinatal Regionalization

Pediatric Cardiology & Transplant

Collaborative clinical research on pediatric heart transplant outcomes, cardiac MRI, Fontan physiology, and pulmonary vein stenosis — developing biostatistical methods and analytic infrastructure for outcomes research in the Lurie Heart Center.

  • Heart Transplant
  • Cardiac MRI
  • Fontan Physiology
  • Pulmonary Vein Stenosis
  • Pediatric Cardiology
