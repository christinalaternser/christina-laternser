About

Quantitative research on how institutions, regulation, and care systems shape health

I am a biostatistician at Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children’s Hospital of Chicago and an economist by training. My work bridges collaborative clinical research and an independent health-systems research agenda, using quantitative methods to examine how institutions, regulation, and the organization of care shape health outcomes and access.

My independent research spans two areas. In pharmaceutical industrial organization, I study how patent strategy and regulatory design shape generic competition under the Hatch-Waxman Act. In maternal health and obstetric systems, I examine the structural determinants of service availability, geographic access, and the persistence of maternity-care vulnerability.

In collaborative clinical research, I work primarily in pediatric cardiology and congenital heart disease outcomes, with a focus on prenatal diagnosis, intervention timing, perinatal regionalization, and disparities in care. My methodological work includes panel econometrics, generalized structural equation models, competing-risks methods, and geospatial analysis.

I received my Ph.D. in Economics from the University of Illinois Chicago, where my dissertation examined pharmaceutical firm behavior under the Hatch-Waxman regulatory framework.

Research Programs

Independent scholarship and collaborative clinical research

My independent work examines institutional design in pharmaceutical and maternal-health markets. My clinical collaborations apply rigorous statistical methods to questions in congenital and pediatric cardiovascular care.

Independent Research

Research programs that I originate and lead as part of my independent scholarly agenda.

Pharmaceutical Industrial Organization

I study how regulatory architecture shapes strategic behavior in pharmaceutical markets. My work examines Orange Book patent-listing strategy, protection regimes, and the timing of generic competition under the Hatch-Waxman Act.

  • Hatch-Waxman Act
  • Generic Entry Timing
  • Patent Strategy
  • Regulatory Design
  • Orange Book
  • Protection Regimes

Maternal Health Access & Obstetric Systems

I examine the structural determinants of obstetric service availability and geographic access. This work distinguishes feasibility from vulnerability, traces changes in county service environments, and measures the population burden of maternity-care absence.

  • Maternity Care Deserts
  • Obstetric Service Regimes
  • Prenatal Care Access
  • Geographic Barriers
  • Rural Health
  • Perinatal Regionalization

Collaborative Clinical Research

Methodological and substantive collaborations within pediatric cardiology and congenital heart disease research.

Congenital Heart Disease Outcomes

In collaborative work with the Lurie Heart Center, I study disparities in the care of children with congenital heart defects — with a focus on prenatal diagnosis, insurance-based differences in intervention timing, length of stay, and perinatal regionalization for critical CHD.

  • Prenatal Diagnosis
  • CHD Outcomes
  • Insurance Disparities
  • Competing Risks
  • Perinatal Regionalization

Pediatric Cardiology & Transplant

Collaborative clinical research on pediatric heart transplant outcomes, cardiac MRI, Fontan physiology, and pulmonary vein stenosis — developing biostatistical methods and analytic infrastructure for outcomes research in the Lurie Heart Center.

  • Heart Transplant
  • Cardiac MRI
  • Fontan Physiology
  • Pulmonary Vein Stenosis
  • Pediatric Cardiology
